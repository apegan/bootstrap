Shifting Rhythms

Shifting Rhythms is an endeavor to co-create a mobile hands-on education space with youth and community leaders in Coahoma County, Mississippi. The mobile trailer travels around Coahoma County to bring hands-on learning experiences and twenty-first century skills to youth through technology, entrepreneurship, and arts training. This effort began in 2013 as a project in Affordable Design and Entrepreneurship (ADE), a course at Olin College dedicated to working with communities to develop projects with social impact. ADE follows a similiar model as MIT's D-Lab. From 2015-2017, I spent several semesters and a summer on the project to grow the project by surveying over 300 students, creating and testing a guitar-building curriculum, collaborating with partners, and securing over 60K in grant funding. The project now has two educational directors teaching dozens of students.
