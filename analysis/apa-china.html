Urban Planning Trends in US Cities

As the Environmental Analyst intern for the American Planning Association branch in China, I analyzed common themes and strategies for sixty U.S. cities of different sizes to present to Chinese city officials. I used a text analysis tool, Semantria, to run through the citywide strategic plans of big, medium, and small cities. I compared the visions, current strategies, and new aspects.

Disciplines

Urban Planning Research Text Analysis Data Visualization

Year

2015

For most cities, establishing a strategic plan was a task completed every five or ten years. Oftentimes, the goals have unclear indicators and metrics, and most cities do not reflect on why goals were or were not accomplished. When the five year mark approached, most cities craft a completely new vision.
After running Semantria for 20 big, 20 medium, and 20 small cities, I learned the most common themes were economic opportunity and preserving a national environment.
Common buzzwords for cities were complete streets, green space, and environmental stewardship.
