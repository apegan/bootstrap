Intended Nationally Determined Contributions for Brazil

For a class exercise, we measured the carbon baseline scenario for Brazil and compared it to our projected mitigation scenarios. The process was similar to how countries propose their intended nationally determined contributions during the Conference of Parties. Faculty at the Stockholm Environment Institute advised us and provided data through a software tool called LEAP, short for Long-range Energy Alternatives Planning System. We aimed to reduce the country's greenhouse gas emission by 15% by the year 2050 relative to 2005. We increased renewable energies, decreasing forest fires, and increasing waste management efficiency due to Brazil's high hydropower and biomass capacity.
We identified the biggest drivers for carbon emission. We learned that in Brazil, population growth, deforestation, and iron production were key drivers.
After crafting policy proposals to address what we thought was a reasonable goal, we learned that our projections were nearly unattainable.
